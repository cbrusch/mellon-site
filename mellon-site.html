Overview

Working in collaboration with the Dean of the Office of the Faculty, the Brown University Library is pleased to introduce a new Digital Publishing Initiative. Supported by a generous grant from the Andrew W. Mellon Foundation, this new Initiative offers faculty members in the humanities and social sciences the opportunity to explore new and innovative approaches to scholarly publishing and research.

In this historical moment in which the traditional form of scholarly publication—particularly the academic monograph—is being thoroughly examined, challenged, and re-imagined, this Digital Publishing Initiative will insert Brown into a much larger cultural conversation through the development of ground-breaking digital publications. Drawing on the rigorous and visionary research that is happening all around the Brown campus, the publishing initiative will develop partnerships between Brown faculty and Library staff, and establish processes that will allow scholarship to be conducted and shared in new ways.

Following the University’s significant history of digital innovation, and building upon the current work of the Library’s Center for Digital Scholarship (CDS), this Initiative adds editorial and design expertise to an already-robust team of digital librarians, technologists, and digital humanists. Brown faculty members in the humanities and social sciences are encouraged to submit an expression of interest, outlining a concept for a digital publication drawn from new or ongoing research.

Proposals will be evaluated by the Digital Publications Advisory Board, and will be assessed on the quality of their scholarly promise, faculty readiness, and digital innovation. Chosen faculty members will work in consultation with the Library’s CDS team, the Digital Scholarship Editor, and the Designer for Online Publications to develop their digital publication project. Faculty will receive editorial, design, and technical support, as well as a single course release in order to pursue their digital publication project.

For more information about this exciting new Initiative, please read the call for expressions of interest on the Information for Faculty page; learn about our Digital Publications Advisory Board; and peek at our Current Projects.
